# About

InnerShell Labs is an independent security research space authored by Diego Concha, focused on understanding how modern systems behave under adversarial conditions.

I work in cybersecurity (since 2020) with a strong focus on offensive security, risk analysis, and the evaluation of complex systems across infrastructure, applications, and distributed architectures.

Over time, my work has evolved from identifying isolated vulnerabilities to analyzing how systems fail as a whole, particularly across authentication, authorization, and trust boundaries.

This site reflects that shift.

## What you’ll find here

The content published in InnerShell Labs focuses on:

- Security engineering and system-level security analysis  
- Authentication and authorization design failures  
- Adversary behavior and attack surface analysis  
- Real-world patterns in vulnerabilities and incidents  
- Risk-driven security thinking and decision-making  

Rather than focusing on tools or isolated findings, this space explores how design decisions influence security outcomes in real systems.

## Approach

The work shared here is guided by a few core principles:

- **Depth over volume** — fewer topics, explored in detail  
- **Fundamentals over hype** — focus on how systems actually work  
- **Systems thinking over checklists** — understanding behavior over applying templates  

## Background

My experience includes ~6 years working on offensive security engagements, Red Team activities, and Cyber Threat Intelligence, supporting organizations across different industries in identifying and understanding their security risks.

This includes:

- Security assessments of web applications, APIs, and infrastructure  
- Analysis of adversary techniques and real-world attack patterns  
- Supporting remediation efforts through risk-based prioritization
